# Heat Smoke And Asthma
Summary: Heat waves, wildfire smoke, and asthma triggers can combine into fast-moving family health problems.

Wildfire smoke, heat wave exposure, and asthma can worsen cough, wheezing, shortness of breath, dizziness, and dehydration faster than families expect.

Good first-line guidance includes moving to cleaner indoor air when possible, cooling the body, checking rescue inhaler access, and recognizing when symptoms are no longer safe to monitor at home.

Transport barriers matter because smoke and heat emergencies often unfold during broader disruptions when driving, cooling, and clinic access are already strained.
