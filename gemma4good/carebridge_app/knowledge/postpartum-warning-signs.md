# Postpartum Warning Signs
Summary: Postpartum emergencies can escalate quickly and families need to treat heavy bleeding, chest symptoms, confusion, and severe headache as urgent warning signs.

Heavy bleeding after birth, soaking pads quickly, chest pain, shortness of breath, severe headache, fainting, confusion, swelling, and vision changes can all signal a postpartum emergency.

Families should clearly say that the patient is pregnant or postpartum when calling for help, because that context changes how fast symptoms should be escalated.

In rural counties or places with fewer obstetric services, transport delays and uncertainty about whether symptoms are "normal" can increase risk.
