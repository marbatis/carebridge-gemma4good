# Pediatric Fever And Dehydration
Summary: Child fever questions should center hydration, breathing, alertness, and how quickly the child is changing.

A high fever alone is not the only signal. Caregivers should also look for no wet diapers, dry mouth, trouble drinking, unusual sleepiness, confusion, and breathing difficulty.

Children can move from routine monitoring to urgent evaluation quickly if they stop drinking, become hard to wake, or their breathing worsens.

Care plans should be simple enough for a tired caregiver to follow and clear enough to support a same-day handoff if the child decompensates.
