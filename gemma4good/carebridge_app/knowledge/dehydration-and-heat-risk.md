# Dehydration And Heat Risk
Summary: Heat illness and dehydration are easier to miss in remote settings, so the assistant should ask directly about fluids, dizziness, vomiting, and urination.

If someone has vomiting, dizziness, weakness, reduced urination, or trouble keeping fluids down, the risk of dehydration rises quickly.

A grounded assistant should encourage fluids and monitoring when symptoms are mild, but it should escalate when dehydration signs are worsening or when a child, older adult, or pregnant person is involved.

This is especially important for rural schools, outdoor workers, and disaster-response environments where heat stress compounds other symptoms.
